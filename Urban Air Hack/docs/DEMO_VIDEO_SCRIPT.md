# VAYU-NET — 3-Minute Demo Video Script

**Setup:** screen-record vayu-net-ten.vercel.app in a maximized browser. Calm pace. One take per scene is fine.

## Scene 1 — The hook (0:00–0:25) · show: Delhi map with red station dots
> "This is Delhi during the New Year smog crisis — real government sensor data, PM2.5 fifty times the safe limit. India has over 900 of these sensors. A government audit found that in 69% of cities, nothing happens when they turn red. VAYU-NET is the layer that makes something happen."

## Scene 2 — The replay (0:25–1:10) · click ▶ Run war-room replay
> "Watch the whole response happen live. A watchdog spots 15 pollution spikes... an attribution engine identifies fireworks and traffic — corroborated by NASA satellite fire data... a forecaster predicts the next 24 hours... enforcement ranks the ten worst wards with legal basis attached... and citizen alerts go out. Total time: under two seconds. The real-world equivalent took days — when it happened at all."

## Scene 3 — Evidence, not opinions (1:10–1:50) · Actions tab → open an Evidence pack PDF
> "Every recommendation is backed by evidence an inspector can hold: the ward's pollution chart, a wind rose showing where it came from, the exact clues — particle ratios, gas signatures, satellite fire anomalies — the schools and hospitals at risk, and the section of law that applies. No black box: the AI never invents a verdict, it scores measurable signals."

## Scene 4 — Honest accuracy (1:50–2:20) · Metrics tab
> "We publish our own report card. Our 24-hour forecast beats the standard baseline by 34% — tested on the hardest window we have. Our source attribution is compared, on screen, against the Supreme Court-mandated government inventory — including where and why an episode week differs. Every coordinate is cross-validated across two independent sources."

## Scene 5 — Scale + close (2:20–3:00) · Compare tab, then Forecast grid toggle
> "Three cities today — same engine, same formula, directly comparable. A fourth city onboards with one boundary file. In December, the Bombay High Court demanded exactly this system for Mumbai — it's still being designed. Ours is deployed, open-source, on data India already collects. India measured its air for a decade. VAYU-NET is what acting on it looks like."
