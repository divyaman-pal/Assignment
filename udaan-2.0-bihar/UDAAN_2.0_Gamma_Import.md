## The Growth–Development Paradox — and Why Now

- Bihar grows fast but stays poor: GSDP Rs 9,91,997 cr in 2024-25 (8.6% real vs 6.5% national), yet per-capita NSDP is Rs 36,342 — only 31.7% of the national ~Rs 1,14,500.
- Industry never arrived: manufacturing ~7% of GSDP (India ~17%); registered factories fell 3,623 (FY16) → 3,307 (FY23); FDI Oct-2019–Dec-2024 was just $215.76 Mn.
- The cost is human export: ~50 Mn Biharis out-migrate; remittances are the primary income for half of all rural households.
- Why now: stable 5-yr mandate (Nov 2025, 200+/243), Rs 7,500-cr MMRY, National Makhana Board (Rs 476-cr), anchor infra (NIFTEM, AKIC-Gaya, Purnea+Bihta airports, Pirpainti). BIPPP-2025 expires Mar-2026 and needs a successor.

## Root-Cause Analysis (1 of 2) — The System

- Six bottlenecks reinforce each other and converge on one outcome: stalled industrialisation + out-migration.
- LAND: 18–36-month conversion vs 3–6 in Gujarat/TN; fragmented 0.39-ha holdings; flood-prone; acquisition unreformed. Hurts investor + job-seeker.
- POWER: electrification ≠ 24×7 industrial supply; captive backup adds +15–20% to unit cost. Hurts MSMEs.
- LOGISTICS: 30–40% post-harvest loss; EDFC under-used; produce rots before market. Hurts marginal farmer.
- Takeaway: these physical gaps starve greenfield projects and rot produce before market — capital and labour leave together.

## Root-Cause Analysis (2 of 2) — Institutional Constraints + the Fix Map

- SKILLS: 58% under 30, but 80% of out-migrants are low-skilled; weak ITIs; very low female labour-force participation. Hurts youth + women.
- FINANCE: 53.5% credit-deposit ratio (banks export Bihar's deposits); MSME credit gap of Rs 1.5–2 lakh cr. Hurts MSME + scaling Didi.
- GOVERNANCE (meta): no true single window; Rs 6,800 bn approved proposals un-operationalised; 9.2%-of-GSDP fiscal deficit (FY24). Hurts everyone.
- Diagnosis: an execution-architecture failure, not a deficit of policy intent.

## Solution 1 — Land & Plug-and-Play Readiness (P1)

- Audience served: local job-seekers + investors. Proven model: Sri City / Tamil Nadu ready-park + anchor. Live rail: BIADA + 10 new parks + AKIC-Gaya.
- Months 1–6: designate 5 Priority Industrial Districts (Gaya, Muzaffarpur, Darbhanga, Bhagalpur, Begusarai), 500–1,000 acres each, govt land first, 90-day land-allotment SLA.
- Months 7–18: build plug-and-play sheds (power/water/ETP/solar feeder); 10 cold stores; stand up 2 Mega Food Parks.
- Months 19–24: reach 60% shed occupancy; 1 anchor tenant per park; captive solar live.
- 24-month target: 60% shed occupancy + 1 anchor per park. Sector lever: light engineering & electronics — AKIC-Gaya PCB/EMS + defence corridor; wages 30–40% below Mumbai/Chennai.

## Solution 2 — Women-Led & Returnee Enterprise Engine (P2)

- Audience served: 1.4 cr JEEViKA women + ~50 Mn migrants. Proven model: Amul + SEWA/Lijjat + Kerala NORKA. Live rail: JEEViKA + MMRY + Makhana Board FPOs + new Returnee Mission.
- Months 1–6: form 200+ JEEViKA producer groups; launch a Migrant & Returnee Registry; map returnee skills to anchor demand.
- Months 7–18: MMRY follow-on credit up to Rs 2 lakh; "Homecoming" loan; creches + safe transport for women lines.
- Months 19–24: commission FPO-owned processing units; scale Lakhpati Didi enterprises; wage-parity + owner tracks to retain returnees.
- 24-month target: 1 lakh Lakhpati Didi + 10,000 returnee units. Sector lever: agro-processing — 8–12 jobs per Rs 1 cr, 60–70% female; makhana processed in-state = 3–4x the jobs of raw.

## Solution 3 — Distributed Cluster Activation / OBOP (P3)

- Audience served: weavers, artisans + district MSMEs. Proven model: UP ODOP + Tirupur RMG + Sialkot CFCs. Live rail: OBOP + District Industries Centres.
- Months 1–6: one product per block (Bhagalpur silk, Madhubani, footwear); design Common Facility Centres; map cluster demand + buyers.
- Months 7–18: commission CFCs on PPP; secure GI tags; onboard clusters to ONDC.
- Months 19–24: activate 15–20 clusters with live orders; link to OBOP marketing; aggregate exports via district hubs.
- 24-month target: 15–20 OBOP clusters with live orders. Sector lever: textiles/apparel/handloom — 50–80 jobs per Rs 1 cr (Bhagalpuri silk GI); leather & footwear 50,000–100,000 jobs in 24 months.

## Solution 4 — Skills-to-Job Pipeline (P4)

- Audience served: ITI youth + skilled diaspora. Proven model: German dual-VET + Vietnam pre-trained cohorts. Live rail: 1,497 ITIs + Kaushal Vikas Kendras.
- Months 1–6: audit all ITIs; invest in the top 300; add 5 demand-led trades; anchor curricula to live employer needs.
- Months 7–18: stand up 5 District Skill Academies (PPP); 6-month apprenticeship link; reserve 40% of seats for women.
- Months 19–24: pre-train cohorts to named-anchor specs; place into park anchors + clusters; track placements to 70%.
- 24-month target: 200,000 certifications · 70% placement. Sector lever: pre-trained cohorts de-risk anchors (China+1 / Vietnam playbook) and unlock female workforce entry.

## Solution 5 — Finance & Governance Acceleration (P5)

- Audience served: MSMEs + investors. Proven model: Telangana TS-iPASS + CGTMSE/SIDBI. Live rail: statutory single window + Credit Guarantee Fund.
- Months 1–6: legislate a statutory single window (45/90-day deemed clearance + public dashboard); seed a Rs 500-cr MSME Credit Guarantee Fund (20% first-loss → ~5x lending).
- Months 7–18: secure a 65% C-D-ratio commitment from banks; launch a Rs 1,000-cr Bihar Industrial Growth Fund.
- Months 19–24: District Industrial Task Forces under DMs; CMO Investment Activation Office runs 60-day health-checks on the Rs 6,800-bn pipeline.
- 24-month target: 45/90-day single-window SLA + 65% C-D ratio. Sector lever: the meta-fix — unblocks the Rs 6,800-bn stalled pipeline and closes the Rs 1.5–2 lakh-cr MSME credit gap.
